An original framework

Theopoetecology. Where God, creativity, and the living earth converge.

An interdisciplinary framework developed by Dennis Matthews Jr. Its thesis: divine meaning flows, the way rain flows through soil, through a living ecosystem of scripture, ecological imagery, poetic language, cultural narrative, and lived experience.

Θεός · Theos

God

Theological reflection, the recognition of divine meaning and the human act of being formed by it.

Ποίησις · Poiesis

Making

The creative act, where imagination, aesthetic form, and embodiment become constitutive of theological knowledge.

Οἶκος · Oikos

Dwelling

The household of creation, the root of ecology itself, where the natural world actively participates in meaning-making.

The Narrative Form

Not “what will happen?” but “who is becoming?” Five stages of ecological maturation, organized around formation rather than conflict.

STAGE 01Germination

Seed

A calling, question, crisis, or symbolic image that generates the narrative, small yet containing the potential for the entire arc. Divine initiative; the kingdom in its smallest form.

STAGE 02Underground

Root

Internal struggle, grounding, and unseen formation beneath the surface, the invisible work that makes visible transformation possible. The desert period most narrative models have no category for.

STAGE 03Emergence

Growth

Transformation becomes visible through conflict, movement, exploration, and outward change. Usually the most expansive phase of the narrative.

STAGE 04Ripening

Fruit

The visible outcome, whether realization, reconciliation, wisdom, redemption, or sacrifice. Meaning made tangible, shareable, and formative.

STAGE 05Generativity

Harvest

The story extends beyond itself, into community, generation, and the planting of new seeds for new narratives. The stage conflict-driven models don't have.

⟳Harvest produces Seed. The form is cyclical, and the story ends with a new beginning.

curation model

Each story is scored through four lenses for context, not control. The numbers are tools for orientation, not judgments.

Biblical Alignment

Does a story affirm, distort, or challenge God's moral order as revealed in Scripture? Every story is examined honestly in light of biblical truth.

The Editorial Foundation of Dot 1 News

The Turning
Toward

A Methodology for Christ-Centered Strategic Media
Dennis Matthews Jr. · Brittany Marie Matthews

Journalism was born as a covenant, not a commodity. It existed to make the powerful accountable to the powerless. It was witness in service of the governed, a way for a citizen to know what they could not personally see, so that they might act as a free and responsible moral agent.

When attention became the product, the covenant inverted. The reader ceased to be the one served and became the thing sold. Information stopped serving discernment and began manufacturing reaction.

We name this dominant paradigm diversion, the movement of attention away. Not merely distraction, but a directed pull: away from the true toward the inflammatory, away from the neighbor toward the enemy, away from the durable toward the urgent but empty.

Diversion is not a failure of modern media. It is, increasingly, its design.

The dominant paradigm

Diversion

The turning of attention away. It asks: what will hold the eye?

Our answer

Conversion

A turning toward. It asks: what is worthy of a person's limited and sacred attention?

01
Reaction→Reflection

The diversionary medium rewards the fastest, hottest response. We deliberately slow the reader down with measured headlines and a refusal of the jolt. We ask not how this makes you feel in the next three seconds, but what it means, and what it asks of you.

02
Spectacle→Dignity

Diversion reduces persons to content: the villain to hate, the victim to consume. We hold that every person in every story bears the image of God, including the ones the reader is invited to despise. Contempt is a form of blindness.

03
Fragment→Frame

The feed delivers the world as disconnected shocks, each erasing the last. We supply context, the reason it matters now, and what still remains unknown, restoring to the fragment the frame that makes it intelligible. A fact without a frame is not information. It is ammunition.

04
Noise→Signal

Not everything true is worth your attention today. Curation is a moral act. We select for the consequential, the dignifying, and the formative, and we refuse the sensational but empty, even when it would perform. Restraint is our signature.

05
Information→Revelation

We do not stop at what happened. Through the confessed lens, we invite the reader past information toward revelation, the drawing back of the curtain on what a story discloses about the world, the neighbor, and God. We end not with a verdict, but a question the reader can carry.

There is no neutral witness, and pretending otherwise is itself a deception. All witness is confessed witness, and the honest course is not to hide the standpoint but to declare it. We name where we stand, do the disciplined work of measured and verifiable reporting, and then serve the reader's own discernment rather than commanding their conclusion. We are not building an audience to sell. We are forming a reader to free.
